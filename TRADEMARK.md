# Lenker Trademark Policy

`Lenker` is the project name and identity for the official open-source project,
official builds, and future official services.

The code license lets you fork, modify, and redistribute the code under its
terms. It does not grant permission to misrepresent a fork, service, package, or
build as the official Lenker project.

## Allowed Uses

- Refer to Lenker when describing compatibility, forks, patches, or deployments.
- Fork the project under the code license.
- Use phrases such as "Lenker fork", "based on Lenker", or "compatible with
  Lenker" when the relationship is clear.

## Not Allowed

- Present a fork or hosted service as the official Lenker project.
- Use the Lenker name, logo, or confusingly similar branding for fraud,
  phishing, malware, or deceptive VPN services.
- Imply endorsement by the Lenker maintainers without explicit permission.
- Use Lenker branding in a way that hides material changes from users or
  providers.

Official builds and future official services should be clearly separated from
community forks. Community forks should use their own service names and make the
relationship to Lenker explicit.

This policy is intentionally simple for the early-stage project and may be
clarified later.
